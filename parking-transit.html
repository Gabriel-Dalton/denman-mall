Getting Here

Denman Place Mall sits at 1030 Denman Street in Vancouver’s West End. Buses, e-scooters, bike-share, car-share and the Seaside path all meet at our front door — here’s the full picture.

Open in Google Maps TransLink trip planner Directions from here Find a Lime nearby

At a glance

  • 3 frequent bus routes stop within a 2-minute walk (#5, #6, #19).
  • Lime e-scooters & e-bikes are usually parked along Denman St and Beach Ave.
  • Seaside Bike Route & Mobi bike-share are two blocks south.
  • 10-minute walk to Stanley Park & English Bay Beach.
  • Evo & Modo car-share vehicles are within a block.
  • Burrard SkyTrain is a 15-minute walk or a 6-minute bus ride.

Public Transit

TransLink buses run along Denman, Davie and Robson Streets, putting the mall within a 2-minute walk of three Frequent Transit Network routes. Pay with a Compass card, contactless credit card or a single-trip ticket from any bus stop pole reader.

Lime, Mobi & Car-share

Denman Street is one of Vancouver’s busiest shared-mobility corridors. You’ll usually find Lime e-scooters, Lime e-bikes and Mobi pedal bikes within a minute of the mall — plus Evo and Modo car-share vehicles a block away.

Lime e-scooters & e-bikes

Micro-mobility

Lime operates pay-as-you-go electric scooters and electric bikes throughout the West End. Denman Street and the Beach Avenue / Seaside path see the largest fleet in the neighbourhood — live availability shows in the Lime app.

Designated parking zones near the mall

Denman St @ Comox St — right outside the mall, marked corral on the sidewalk.
Davie St @ Denman St — high-turnover zone next to the crosswalk.
Beach Ave @ Bidwell St — on the seawall, ideal after a beach stop.
Robson St @ Denman St — for trips north toward Stanley Park.

How it works

  • Download the Lime app and add a payment method.
  • Scan the QR code on the scooter or e-bike to unlock.
  • Helmets are required in BC — bring your own or use the helmet stored on the bike where provided.
  • Ride on the road or in bike lanes, not on sidewalks.
  • End your trip inside a green zone in the app to avoid a parking fee.

Pricing varies by city and promotion — check the Lime app for the current unlock fee and per-minute rate. Lime offers a Lime Access discount for qualifying riders.

Car-share

The West End is a Home Zone for both major car-share services — vehicles are usually parked on the street within a block of the mall.

Available services

  • Evo — free-floating, park anywhere in the Home Zone. evo.ca →
  • Modo — round-trip co-op cars at fixed locations. modo.coop →

Need to drop someone off? The Comox Street curb beside the mall is a convenient passenger pick-up/drop-off zone.

Cycling & Walking

Cycling

The mall is two blocks from the Seaside Bike Route — Vancouver’s 28 km separated waterfront path that loops Stanley Park, English Bay, False Creek and Granville Island.

  • Free covered bike parking outside the main Denman St entrance.
  • Comox–Helmcken Greenway connects east toward Yaletown and BC Place.
  • Beach Ave bike lane is protected from traffic the whole way to Stanley Park.
  • Cycling helmets are required by law in BC.

Walking

Denman is one of the city’s most walkable streets — tree-lined, flat and lined with cafes.

Driving Here

From Downtown

Take Robson, Davie or Pacific Street westbound and turn onto Denman.

From the North Shore

Cross the Lions Gate Bridge, follow the Stanley Park Causeway to West Georgia Street, then turn south on Denman.

Parking

Adjacent paid parking lot

A paid surface parking lot sits next to the mall, accessed from the Nelson Street side of the building.

The parking lot is independently operated.

The lot is owned and managed by a third-party operator (signage on site shows the current operator and contact details). Denman Place Mall does not own, operate, or control this lot. We cannot issue refunds, void tickets, validate parking, adjust rates, or resolve disputes. For any parking issue — payment, tickets, towing, lost cars — please contact the parking-lot operator directly using the signage in the lot.

Directions to the lot

Metered street parking

Paid metered parking is available along Denman, Nelson, Comox and nearby side streets. Rates and time limits are set by the City of Vancouver. PayByPhone works at every meter.

City of Vancouver parking info →

Accessible parking

Designated accessible parking is available in the adjacent lot and on nearby streets. Holders of a valid SPARC BC accessible-parking permit can park free at any City of Vancouver meter, subject to posted time limits.

Read our Accessibility Statement →

Questions?

For questions about reaching the mall, see our Contact page. For questions about parking charges, tickets or refunds in the adjacent lot, please contact the parking-lot operator directly using the information posted on lot signage — the mall is not able to help with those.
